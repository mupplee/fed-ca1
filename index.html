Promoting Sustainable Development Goal 7: Ensuring Access to Affordable and Clean Energy

The Sustainable Development Goal 7 (SDG 7) set by the United Nations focuses on providing sustainable and modern energy access for everyone. This objective plays a role in tackling issues like climate change, poverty, and economic gcolumnth.

Types of sustainable power

Solar energy

Solar power is a popular and commonly used sustainable energy source for rural electrification. Solar home systems (SHS) typically include solar panels, batteries, inverters, and charge controllers. These systems are simple to install, adaptable, and low-maintenance, making them excellent for both personal residences and small enterprises. They can provide critical services like lighting, phone charging, and powering tiny equipment in schools and clinics.

Wind Energy

Wind energy, particularly the usage of small wind turbines, is another renewable source that is ideal for rural electrification. Small wind turbines are made up of wind turbines, inverters, and battery storage devices. These systems work well in locations with constant wind patterns and can be utilised on their own or in conjunction with other energy sources.

Micro-Hydropower

Micro-hydropower systems, particularly run-of-river installations, are extremely successful in rural settings near rivers or streams. These systems use water turbines, generators, and control systems to generate power from the natural flow of water while causing minimal environmental impact. Micro-hydropower is dependable and can generate regular power, making it a great choice for towns and small settlements.

Real Life Example: Utilizing Solar Power in Rural Areas

An instance of advancing SDG 7 involves the use of solar energy in remote rural communities worldwide. Many isolated regions lack access to energy grids, posing challenges for residents to power their residences, schools, and businesses. Nevertheless, the decreasing costs of panels and the improved efficiency of technology have rendered it a feasible solution for these communities.

Improvements

Organizations such as Solar Aid and Barefoot College are actively engaged in providing solar-powered lighting and electricity to households in off-grid regions. These efforts not only offer energy but also generate employment opportunities for local individuals who are trained to install and upkeep the solar systems.

The impact of these initiatives extends beyond. Children can study after sunset, medical facilities can store life-saving medications under refrigeration, and small enterprises can operate effectively. By harnessing energy's potential, these communities are taking strides toward a sustainable future.
